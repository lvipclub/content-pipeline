DATA CENTRE COOLING — FIELD REFERENCE

Every kilowatt that enters a data centre follows a simple rule: what is not consumed by computing equipment must be paid for in cooling.

As of 2026, AI training workloads push rack densities past 25 kW — territory where conventional air cooling hits its ceiling. Here's what facilities engineers need to know.

——

PUE = Ptotal ÷ PIT

The metric, defined by The Green Grid. A PUE of 1.0 means zero overhead. 2.0 means every watt of computing costs another watt in facility power.

Typical ranges:
• Air-cooled legacy: 1.6–2.0
• Well-managed enterprise (containment + DCIM): 1.2–1.4
• Liquid-cooled hyperscale: 1.03–1.15

PUE is not static — it fluctuates with IT load and outdoor conditions. Real-time measurement via IPMI, PMBus, and rack PDU monitoring is essential.

——

RACK PLACEMENT & AIRFLOW

Before liquid cooling — fix the air path. These are prerequisites, not optional:

1. Hot aisle / cold aisle configuration — prevents supply/return mixing. Enables densities from ~5 kW to 25 kW.
2. Physical containment — doors, chimney returns, end caps. Eliminates bypass and recirculation.
3. Under-floor pressure management — DCIM-integrated transducers + variable-speed fans for active balancing.

——

LIQUID COOLING vs AIR COOLING

The heat transfer coefficient of liquid is orders of magnitude higher than air.

| Factor | Air Cooling | Liquid Cooling |
|---|---|---|
| Max rack density | 15–25 kW | 50–100+ kW |
| Typical PUE | 1.4–2.0 | 1.03–1.15 |
| Capex | Low | High |
| Opex (cooling) | 30–50% of facility | 3–15% of facility |
| Water consumption | High (evaporative) | Lower (closed-loop) |

Water-cooled solutions reduce PUE from >1.6 to <1.1. The lowest PUE values in the industry are consistently associated with liquid cooling — driven by improved approach temperatures and more efficient pumping.

——

WHAT ASHRAE TC09.09 SAYS

• Densities increase from 5 kW → 10, 15, 25 kW+. Beyond ~20 kW, air becomes uneconomical.
• At 25 kW/rack, thermal runaway window collapses to seconds. PMBus and DCIM are no longer elective.
• Liquid cooling facilities achieve PUE <1.1; optimised installations approach 1.03.
• DCIM-driven firmware strategies yield an additional 5–15% reduction beyond hardware gains.

——

KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. Measure PUE at the rack level, not the utility meter.
2. Airflow optimisation is cheap and mandatory — do it before any capital-intensive cooling upgrade.
3. The air ceiling is ~20–25 kW/rack. Beyond that, physics does not negotiate.
4. Liquid cooling drives the lowest PUE. The operational savings can offset capex within 2–4 years.
5. Rack PDU data feeds DCIM-driven optimisation — savings without hardware changes.
