**Date:** 2026-07-23

**Hook:**
Building energy codes across Asia are converging on a common trajectory — net-zero ready by 2030-2035. Singapore's BCA Green Mark 2026 update, Hong Kong's BEAM Plus 2.0, and Dubai's Al Sa'fat all now mandate submetering, continuous commissioning, and minimum 30% energy performance improvement over baseline. The variance is in enforcement timelines, not ambition.

**Key Talking Points:**
- Submetering per floor/zone is now mandatory in BCA Green Mark 2026 new builds (SG) and BEAM Plus v2.0 major retrofits (HK). Design-stage specification of BACnet-based energy meters avoids costly post-fit.
- Continuous commissioning clauses are appearing in tender documents across the Gulf. This shifts the spec from "install and commission" to "install, commission, and monitor for 3 years" — a fundamental change to the controls specifier's scope.
- ASHRAE 90.1-2025 and EN 16798-1 both push for demand-controlled ventilation tied to energy monitoring. The intersection of IAQ and energy efficiency is now a code requirement, not a premium upgrade.
- Energy audits under ISO 50002 require interval data (15-min granularity). Proprietary BAS protocols that don't export at this granularity are increasingly non-compliant. BACnet/IP and Modbus TCP remain the safe spec.
