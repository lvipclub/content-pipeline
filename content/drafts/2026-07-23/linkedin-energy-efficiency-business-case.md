**Headline:** How Three Asian Building Codes Are Driving a Convergence in Energy Efficiency Specifications

---

**Body:**

The conversation around building energy efficiency has shifted from "why should we?" to "by when?" Building codes across Asia — Singapore's BCA Green Mark 2026, Hong Kong's BEAM Plus v2.0, and Dubai's Al Sa'fat — have all updated within the 2025-2026 cycle to mandate submetering, continuous commissioning, and minimum 30% energy improvement over baseline.

While each code approaches compliance differently, the specification implications are remarkably consistent:

**Submetering is now mandatory.** BCA Green Mark 2026 requires per-floor or per-zone energy submetering for new commercial buildings. BEAM Plus v2.0 extends this to major retrofits. For controls specifiers, this means BACnet-based energy meters must be designed into the BAS from the schematic phase — retrofitting submetering post-occupancy is 3-5x more expensive and often incompatible with proprietary BAS protocols.

**Continuous commissioning is changing the contract model.** Gulf projects increasingly include a 3-year monitoring-and-commissioning period in the controls tender. The specifier's deliverable shifts from "system commissioned at handover" to "system performing to design intent for 36 months." This favours open-protocol BAS platforms that produce auditable trend logs at 15-minute intervals [ISO 50002].

**IAQ and energy efficiency are converging.** ASHRAE 90.1-2025 and EN 16798-1 both mandate demand-controlled ventilation tied to real-time occupancy data. The old fixed-outdoor-air-fraction approach satisfies neither code. CO₂-based DCV that maintains indoor air quality while reducing outdoor air treatment load by 30-40% is now the minimum standard [ASHRAE 62.1-2022].

For consultants and specifiers working across markets, the message is clear: design for BACnet/IP or Modbus TCP, specify continuous monitoring capability, and budget for post-handover commissioning services. The codes are converging — and the specification language you write today determines whether your project is compliant in 2030.
