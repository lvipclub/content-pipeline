# Continuous IAQ Monitoring Is Now Table Stakes for Class A Office Buildings

In 2026, indoor air quality monitoring has moved from "nice-to-have" to tenant expectation in commercial real estate. Across New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Phoenix, Class A office buildings now track CO₂, PM2.5, and TVOCs the same way they track energy consumption and occupancy [COR Advisors, 2026].

The shift is not regulatory — it's commercial. Tenants negotiating leases now ask for air quality data. WELL certification has grown 5x since 2022. And ASHRAE's publication of Standard 241 (Control of Infectious Aerosols) in 2023 created a new compliance benchmark that facility managers cannot ignore.

## The Numbers Behind the Trend

- **Productivity:** Elevated CO₂ (945 ppm vs 550 ppm) correlates with a 15% decline in cognitive function scores — across crisis response, information usage, and strategy [Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, 2015]
- **Energy:** Demand-controlled ventilation (DCV) based on real-time CO₂ sensing can reduce outdoor air intake by 30-40% during partial occupancy, with sensor costs recovered within 18-24 months [ASHRAE 62.1 User's Manual]
- **Compliance:** ASHRAE 62.1-2022 Addendum ab now explicitly permits CO₂-based DCV when sensors are placed in the breathing zone — closing a long-standing ambiguity that made some engineers hesitant to specify sensor-driven ventilation strategies

## What Specifiers Should Require in Performance Briefs

1. **POE sensors with open protocol output** — BACnet or Modbus. Avoid proprietary wireless ecosystems that lock the building into one vendor's cloud.
2. **CO₂ + PM2.5 + TVOC as minimum parameters** — not just CO₂ alone. Particulate matter from unfiltered outdoor air and VOCs from off-gassing materials contribute independently to occupant complaints.
3. **Breathing-zone placement** — sensors mounted at 1.1-1.7 m height in occupied zones, not in return air ducts where CO₂ is diluted.
4. **Integration with the BMS** — sensor data that lives in a separate dashboard is useless for automated ventilation control. Push data to the building automation system where sequences can act on it.

## Cross-Border Relevance

For firms operating across Asia-Pacific and the Middle East: Hong Kong's BEAM Plus, Singapore's Green Mark, and Dubai's Al Sa'fat rating system now align IAQ credits with WELL v2 benchmarks. A sensor spec written once can serve projects from Kowloon to Kuala Lumpur without modification — provided you avoid region-locked cloud platforms.

## Bottom Line

IAQ monitoring is not an amenity. It's an operational asset that pays for itself through energy savings, tenant retention, and compliance. The hardware is commodity; the integration strategy is where your project wins or loses.
